Epilog is a computer language based on Dynamic Logic Programming (DLP). Epilog is similar to Prolog in many ways, but there are some important differences. The syntax and the semantics of view definitions are essentially the same in both languages. However, the languages differ in the handling of side effects. Whereas Prolog treats side effects implicitly using assert and retract as subgoals in view definitions, Epilog enables users to express side effects explicitly in the form of transition rules.

The first Epilog interpreter was written in Lisp in the late 1990s, and since then it has been ported to Java and Javascript. Over the years, Epilog has been used in a variety of applications, including digital hardware simulation, diagnosis, and testing, data integration, enterprise management, computational law, and general game playing.
